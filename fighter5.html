Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel was born in Nadiad, Gujarat, on October 31, 1875 (His birth anniversary is now observed as National Unity Day or Rashtriya Ekta Diwas). Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel was a senior leader of the Indian National Congress and a prominent figure in the Indian Freedom Struggle, who later became Indiaâ€™s first Deputy Prime Minister and first Home Minister. He played a significant role in organizing and mobilizing the Kheda Satyagraha of 1918, which was spearheaded by Gandhi to protest against the high taxes forced by the British on farmers despite crop failure owing to heavy rains and the plague epidemic. In 1920, the Congress started the non-cooperation struggle and Vallabhbhai gave up his practice. He setup the Gujarat Vidyapeeth where children could study instead of attending Government schools. In 1928 he successfully organized the landowners of Bardoli against British tax increases. It was after this that Vallabhbhai was given the title of Sardar (Leader). He played a crucial role in the torturous negotiations with the British for freedom and Partition of the country. Vallabhbhai Patel was one of the first Congress leaders to accept the partition of India as a solution to the rising Muslim separatist movement led by Muhammad Ali Jinnah.
